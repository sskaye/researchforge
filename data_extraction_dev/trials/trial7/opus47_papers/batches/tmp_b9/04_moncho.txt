                                                                           ■    CONCLUSIONS
                                                                           Our ﬁndings demonstrate that the α-ﬂuorocarbocation
                                                                           resulting from the addition of F−X (X = F, OAc, OTFA,
                                                                           and the like) to double bonds is a unique moiety, quite distinct
                                                                           from common carbocations. Its short life span is responsible
                                                                           for some unexpected reactions, such as the syn-addition across
                                                                           double bonds. When, however, the reaction’s transition state
                                                                           does not favor a parallel approach to the double bond as in the
                                                                           cases described herein, the very short living α-ﬂuorocarboca-
                                                                           tion intermediate may be responsible for unconventional
